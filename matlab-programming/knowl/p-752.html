A good rectangle of c-values to look at is the one with real parts between -2.25 and 1.75 and imaginary parts between -1.5 and 1.5. You want to divide this viewing rectangle up into individual “pixels”, something like 800 by 600. The best way to do so is to have two 800x600 arrays - one for the x-values and one for the y-values. You can then simply create all c-values that you are interested in via \(c = x+i*y\) (Matlab knows complex numbers!). You can ‘fill’ these arrays by hand (using a loop), or by using the Matlab meshgrid command.
